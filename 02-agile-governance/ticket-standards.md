# Jira Ticket Standards & Quality Checklist

## Purpose

This document defines the **minimum quality standards** required for all Jira tickets across project and BAU workstreams. Consistent ticket quality ensures:

* Predictable sprint execution
* Accurate reporting and KPIs
* Reduced rework and ambiguity
* Effective cross-team collaboration

The Scrum Master is responsible for **enforcing these standards** and coaching teams toward consistent adoption.

---

## Ticket Types Covered

* Epic
* Story
* Task
* Bug / Defect
* BAU / Operational Request

---

## Mandatory Ticket Fields (All Tickets)

| Field        | Requirement                                    |
| ------------ | ---------------------------------------------- |
| Summary      | Clear, concise, outcome-focused                |
| Description  | Business context and scope                     |
| Priority     | Agreed with Product Owner                      |
| Labels       | Team, Work Type (Project/BAU), Impacted System |
| Assignee     | Named owner before work begins                 |
| Dependencies | Identified and linked where applicable         |

---

## Story & Task Standards

### Summary Format

> *As a [user/system], I want [capability] so that [business value]*

---

### Description Requirements

* Clear explanation of the problem or goal
* Scope boundaries defined
* Assumptions documented

---

### Acceptance Criteria

* Written in testable, verifiable statements
* Clearly define success
* Approved during backlog refinement

---

### Estimation

* Story points assigned by the team
* Estimation reviewed during refinement
* Re-estimation required if scope changes

---

## Bug / Defect Standards

### Required Information

* Clear reproduction steps
* Expected vs actual behavior
* Environment details
* Severity and impact assessment

---

### Classification

* Production vs Non-production
* Security-related vs Functional
* BAU classification applied

---

## BAU Ticket Standards

### BAU Intake Rules

* Business impact defined
* Urgency agreed
* SLA expectations clarified
* Capacity allocation reviewed

---

### BAU Prioritization

* BAU tickets labeled and tracked separately
* BAU work capped to protect sprint commitments
* Escalations reviewed by Scrum Master and PO

---

## Dependency Management Standards

* Dependencies explicitly documented
* Linked tickets used for cross-team visibility
* Dependency risks raised early
* Reviewed during refinement and sprint planning

---

## Ticket Validation Checklist (Scrum Master)

Before a ticket enters a sprint, confirm:

* [ ] Acceptance criteria present and clear
* [ ] Story points assigned
* [ ] Priority agreed
* [ ] Dependencies identified
* [ ] BAU vs Project correctly labeled
* [ ] Meets Definition of Ready

Tickets failing this checklist remain in **Refinement**.

---

## Jira Hygiene Rules

* Tickets updated daily during active work
* Status reflects reality, not intention
* Comments used to capture decisions and blockers
* No orphaned or stalled tickets without visibility

---

## Reporting Impact

Adherence to these standards enables accurate:

* Velocity reporting
* Cycle time measurement
* Defect trend analysis
* Delivery predictability metrics

---

## Ownership

* **Scrum Master:** Governance enforcement and quality control
* **Product Owner:** Priority and business clarity
* **Team:** Accurate execution and updates

---

## Outcome

Consistent ticket standards create a **clean, reliable Jira system** that supports delivery excellence and leadership confidence.
